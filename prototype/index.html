Facilitating successful brainstorming sessions

R541 Major Project - Qian Linlin

Welcome to this self-paced e-learning course about facilitating successful brainstorming session. In the game industry, creativity is always key. It is important for game designers to enable everyone in the team to contribute their best ideas to early game concept development. One important skill for them to acquire is facilitating brainstorming sessions.

Learning objectives
  • Reflect on their past experiences of facilitating brainstorming sessions: what worked well, what didn’t, what contributed to that?
  • Identify what worked well in the Pixar Animation’s brainstorming practice, relating to the success factors introduced in the course
  • Develop authentic work scenarios that require the successful application of facilitation skills for brainstorming sessions
  • Write a plan for a brainstorming session following the checklist provided in the course
    • Who is this for

  • Anyone who needs to lead or facilitate brainstorming sessions at work or life. If you work with creative media such as games, you may find the context and examples more relevant. However, the approaches and tools shared in this course are applicable to all contexts.
  • This course also works well for small groups of two to three people who have shared interest. Your group can discuss and exchange experiences and ideas as you go through the course, especially the discussion exercises

    Part One: Your best brainstorming session

    Introduction To begin with, let's revisit some past brainstorming sessions that you ever attended as a participant. Can you describe a session that worked out particularly well? Use the following questions to guide your reflection.
    1. What was the context and purpose of the session?
    2. How were you feeling in the session?
    3. What results did it achieve?
    4. What contributed to the successful results? try to think of as many details as you can, such as the room, the people, the facilitator, the process, the questions, the methods, ...

    Part Two: Success factors of facilitating an effective brainstorming session

    Introduction Now that you have reflected on some successful brainstorming experiences. Let's take a look at what factors contribute to their successes. In this part, you will learn six success factors with specific examples. As you go through them, think critically how they might work for your particular contexts and audience? While I find these factors useful for most successful sessions, facilitators usually adjust them to particular situations. Click into the following videos by sequence.
